# Duskhaven

**Summary:** Duskhaven is a massive, bustling, and deeply corrupt coastal trade port. While it projects an image of a thriving cosmopolitan hub, it has seen better days. The city operates under a heavy fog of perpetual industrial grime, vast wealth inequality, and rampant organized crime. It is a city of shadows, where the legitimate government is hopelessly entangled with the criminal syndicates.

## City Districts
* **The Docks:** Located on the south coast, this is the industrial heart of the city. It is a maze of towering warehouses, creaking cranes, and heavily guarded caravans.
* **Shadow Quarter:** East of the Docks. A sprawling, neglected slum where the city's poorest residents and underclass races are pushed. The architecture is crumbling and the city watch rarely patrols here.
* **The Veil:** Northwest of the Docks. A labyrinthine district of narrow, twisting streets, sudden dead ends, and perpetual shadows. It is deeply atmospheric, highly dangerous for outsiders, and perfect for clandestine meetings.
* **Market Square:** The commercial and trade hub in the exact center of Duskhaven. A chaotic clash of legitimate merchants, pickpockets, and syndicate fronts.
* **Noble Quarter:** The northwest heights. Home to old money, high-born families, and sprawling, heavily guarded estates. 
* **The High District:** The northeast sector. Home to the newly wealthy, successful merchants, and guild leaders trying to buy their way into nobility.
* **The Sewers:** An extensive, ancient, and deeply hazardous underground network that connects directly to the ocean and the deeper Underdark. It functions as a secondary city for the underclass.
* **Old Ruins:** Located just outside the city. The remains of a long-abandoned ancient Elf city (distinct from Therandril). It is considered deeply cursed and haunted by the locals, heavily avoided by all but the most desperate scavengers.

## Atmosphere & Society (DM Directives)
* **The Vibe:** Play Duskhaven like a noir thriller. It should feel grimy, cynical, and dangerous. Everyone has a price, and legitimate authority is usually just muscle paid for by someone rich.
* **The Underclass:** Standard fantasy races live on the surface, but the "monstrous" and Underdark races are relegated to the literal and societal bottom, confined mostly to the Shadow Quarter, the Docks, and the Sewers. They survive by operating the gray markets and the brutal machinery of the city's crime syndicates.
